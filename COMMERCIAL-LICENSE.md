# Commercial licensing information

> **Informational only; not a licence or contract.** This document describes situations in which
> Vulkan Technologies may offer commercial terms. It does not grant commercial rights, quote
> final terms, or replace advice from a qualified software-licensing attorney.

The Talos Community Edition may be used commercially under `AGPL-3.0-or-later`, provided the user
complies with the AGPL. A commercial licence is an alternative for use cases that need rights or
commitments beyond those open-source terms.

A commercial licence may be appropriate for organizations that want to:

- keep modifications private when the AGPL would require an offer of Corresponding Source;
- embed covered Talos components in a proprietary product;
- redistribute Talos under terms other than the AGPL;
- offer an OEM or white-label distribution;
- obtain enterprise support, maintenance commitments, or service-level agreements;
- negotiate commercial warranties, liability allocation, or indemnification; or
- access separately offered proprietary enterprise modules or integrations.

Whether a commercial licence is needed depends on the proposed architecture, distribution, and
use. This document does not state that every private modification, integration, commercial service,
or hosted offering requires a commercial licence. An organization may instead use the Community
Edition under the AGPL when it can and chooses to meet the applicable obligations.

Commercial scope, fees, support, warranties, indemnities, term, and other conditions are agreed
separately in a written agreement signed by Vulkan Technologies and the customer. No commercial
terms are created by downloading, using, or contributing to this repository.
